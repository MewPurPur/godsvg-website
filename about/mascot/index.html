Meet Vicki, the vector fox mascot of GodSVG

Vicki is GodSVG's official mascot - a curious blue fox that represents precision, flexibility, creativity, and a fascination with the inner workings of things.

If you want to learn more about Vicki, check out her introduction blog post.

GodSVG's logo originated from some quick sketches of G-shaped logos I made to replace the early placeholder one. Things clicked when I realized the fox's head could act as the crossbar. Tom Blackwell iterated on the idea to make the first blue fox logo, from there I iterated a lot more to get the current logo.

A few months after the logo was made, I conceived Vicki as a mascot for the application, deriving from it.

There are no real guidelines for how to draw her, make whatever fanart you want. But if you want to be more faithful, here are the general guidelines for official artworks:

  • Blue coat, darker blue around the paws, lighter blue around the lower half of the face and the tip of the tail.
  • Quadruped build, no anthropomorphization or heavy skewing of the fox anatomy. No heavily exaggerated body proportions.
  • Three or four toes on each paw, anything that looks "paw-like" works, even forepaws resembling hands. No claws.
  • Vicki is energetic, agile, and as intelligent as a human. She can be posed in ways that are physically realistic for a fox, even if they are behaviors a real fox would not naturally perform.
  • The RGB paint trail behind the tail is optional, but should generally be drawn. It should generally not affect the tip of the tail. Droplets of paint dripping from the tail can also be drawn if the tail is raised up. The sequence should always be red-green-blue, but the orientation of it doesn't matter.
  • No stereotypical indicators that Vicki is female.
